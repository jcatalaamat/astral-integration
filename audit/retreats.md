# audit: /retreats (RetreatsPage)

## verdict: strong. interactive stream selector is a highlight.

### what works
- hero line "a retreat booking isn't a hotel reservation" is clear positioning
- interactive offering stream selector (retreats, therapy, homestays, programs, workshops) is the best interactive element on any audience page
- "discovery > intake > preparation > experience > integration" journey flow is thorough
- booking.com comparison cards (they assume / your reality) are effective
- sacred counsel case study is well placed (5 revenue streams, real URL)

### issues

1. **pricing inconsistency** — meta says "12% rev share, Membership tier" but card shows "5-15%"
   - **fix**: show "12%" with "Membership tier" detail

2. **"The Honest Question" section title** — same as every other page
   - **fix**: "The Question Every Center Asks"

3. **journey timeline only highlights step 01** — visually, only the first step gets the accent bg. in the makers page, the numbering system gives each step equal weight. consider making the current active step highlighted, or all of them equally styled.

4. **"each with its own intake, pricing, and client journey" in hero body** — this phrase pattern (X, Y, and Z) appears too many times across the site. fine here, but be aware.

5. **sacred counsel mentions ayahuasca explicitly** — "ayahuasca retreats, integration therapy, private homestays, and a 21-day metabolic detox". this is factual and fine for the case study, but consider whether explicitly naming ayahuasca on a marketing page is the right call given payment processor / advertising restrictions. some stripe and ad platforms flag this.

6. **og url: astralintegration.co** — rebrand

### copy fixes
- pricing card: show "12%" instead of "5-15%"
- note: review ayahuasca mention with jordi re: payment processor risk
